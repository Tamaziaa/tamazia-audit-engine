## Nexus (Rule 13) - required_nexus rationale
- `established_in` only: the pure membership/registration duties that bind the operating agent (UK_LETTINGS_REDRESS_2014 uses established_in + serves_customers_in; UK_CMP_REGS_2019 and UK_MLR_2017_ESTATE_AGENCY use established_in alone) - you are bound because you carry on the regulated work in England, not because a particular customer is served.
- `established_in` + `serves_customers_in`: the customer-facing conduct duties (estate-agency redress and s.18/21 disclosure, CRA fee publication, Tenant Fees, material information, EPC listings) that attach to how the agent deals with consumers in the jurisdiction.
- `processes_residents_of` is deliberately NOT used: these are property conduct-of-business duties, not data-processing (GDPR Art.3) duties, so the data-processing nexus would be the wrong door.
- sub_jurisdiction left null (whole-UK scope) following the uk-legal precedent; the finer England-only vs England-and-Wales operative scope is carried in applies_when/excluded_when (for example the 2014 Order and CMP 2019 are England-only; CRA 2015 s.83 and EPB 2012 are England and Wales).

## Open questions / notes for the adversarial legal-QA pass
1. **DMCCA currency.** The unfair-commercial-practices regime moved from the CPUTRs 2008 to DMCCA 2024 Part 4 on 6 Apr 2025 and NTSELAT withdrew its material-information guidance on 9 May 2025 pending CMA guidance. The underlying s.227 duty is unchanged and cited to the statute; the property-specific material-information heads in the elements are attributed to the NTSELAT/CMA guidance in provenance, not to the statute. Re-check when the CMA publishes its property guidance.
2. **Renters' Rights Act 2025.** Schedule 1 of the Tenant Fees Act shows textual amendments from the Renters' Rights Act 2025 (some in force from 1 May 2026). The permitted/prohibited-payments regime and the GBP 5,000/GBP 30,000 penalties stand; a future pass should confirm no amendment has changed a cited figure.
3. **Enforcement depth.** Only two of eight records carry an official-host enforcement case (MLR, lettings redress). This is honest under the official-host constraint rather than a data gap; local-authority penalty notices under CRA/CMP/Tenant Fees are common in reality but are not published on allowlisted hosts. Consider whether NTSELAT's Bristol City Council host (bristol.gov.uk, an official .gov.uk host) should be added for prohibition-order and penalty grounding in a later pass.
4. **register_url.** Left null where no verified official-host register exists; set to verified GOV.UK guidance/register pages for the lettings-redress, Tenant Fees and MLR records.
