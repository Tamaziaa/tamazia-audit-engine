## (d) MINTING SPEED HISTORY — the 3-parallel-mints-in-45-seconds era

The original operating target was **any website minted in ~45 seconds, with `MINT_CONCURRENCY=3` internal workers draining the whole queue in one `mint-now.yml` run**. That was the baseline through the v8→v17 deterministic era; the pipeline was fast because the load-bearing work was deterministic regex/registry attachment and the crawl was budget-bounded.

**What slowed it down (self-inflicted, flagged honestly in EL-095/096):** the v22.6+ additions — THE LLM GATE with up to 3 escalating attempts, cross-model quorum, self-learning law discovery, register checks, and raised crawl budgets (`MINT_BUILD_TIMEOUT_MS` 720000, 90s fetch pool) — turned mints from ~45s into minutes. The founder called it out ("the ~45s-any-website strategy was lost"). Diagnosis (E-236): the slowdown was NOT the page budget but **serial waiting** — 120 pages at concurrency 14 = ~9 sequential network rounds; the SPA-render tail ran at width 6 with a **45s FLOOR (`Math.max`)** so a few shells always cost ≥45s; sitemap discovery was fully sequential (root then each child, 8s each).

**What restored it (E-236, MEASURED):** pure parallelism with identical maxPages, identical TIER-1 policy-first ordering, identical fallbacks — accuracy unchanged by construction. `anthonygold.co.uk`: **BEFORE >43s (timed out) → AFTER 6.9s, 90 pages, policy_coverage 1.0.** Then E-238 found the residual stall: even at 6.9s crawl, nothing landed because the Gemini free tier was exhausted and every gate attempt walked the chain hitting rate-limited providers, burning `LLM_TIMEOUT_MS` 30s × 3 retries × 3 gate attempts → **minutes waiting for answers that were never coming**, then dropping to deterministic anyway. Fixes: `LLM_TIMEOUT_MS` 30s→12s, an llm-verify deadline (it had none), gate `deadline_ms` budgets (classify 110s / exec 45s / discovery 90s) as CAPS not floors, and law discovery made FIRE-AND-FORGET (learning never spends mint wall-clock). Later, E-268/v23.9 found cookie `observe()` hanging a mint 752s because its 30s goto timeout didn't bound browser launch+networkidle → wrapped in a 35s hard `Promise.race`. Standing principle that emerged: **the budget must be a CAP, not a floor**, and any external step (LLM, browser) must have a hard deadline that fails open.
